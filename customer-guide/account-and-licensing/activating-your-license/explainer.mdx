---
title: "Activating your license"
description: "Activate your BillBasket plan on a new device by signing in with your account, and understand trial vs Pro stores and device slots."
---

BillBasket licenses are tied to your BillBasket account, not to a single
device. Sign in with your account on any new device, and BillBasket checks
your plan and activates automatically — no separate license key to type in,
as long as your plan has a free slot.

## Understanding the fields

These fields all live in **Settings → License & Account**, reached from the
gear icon:

- **Sign in / Pair this device** — the button shown when a device isn't yet
  licensed. Starts the sign-in flow that links this device to your
  BillBasket account.
- **Licensed to** — once signed in, shows your account's business name,
  owner name, mobile number, and email — read-only, coming from your
  account rather than this device.
- **Package** — your current plan name.
- **Trial Stores / Pro Stores** — your plan's usage as "X of Y" for each
  tier. A new store usually starts as a **trial**; **Activate this store**
  converts it to a Pro store using a free Pro slot, if one exists — all
  data is preserved either way. If both your Trial and Pro slots are full,
  activating a new device or store isn't possible until a slot is freed
  (see [Devices & login](/customer-guide/account-and-licensing/devices-and-login/explainer))
  or your plan is upgraded.
- **License status / Valid until / days left** — the offline entitlement
  lease's state and expiry. BillBasket renews this automatically in the
  background every 6 hours while online, so the app keeps working for a
  window of time even without internet — the days-left counter only matters
  if you go a long stretch offline.
- **Upgrade / Manage plan** — opens your BillBasket account online to change
  plans or add slots.

<Note>
  There's no separate "enter a license key" field in the current app — the
  entire flow is account sign-in based. If your plan is at its device
  limit, BillBasket tells you to free up a slot first rather than letting
  you enter a code to bypass it.
</Note>
