---
title: "Importing existing data"
description: "Import your existing products, customers, suppliers, and opening stock into BillBasket from an Excel or CSV spreadsheet using the built-in importer."
---

Import master data (products, customers, suppliers) from an Excel file
under **Settings → Data Import/Export**. Download the template first to
match the expected columns.

## Understanding the fields

- **Import template** — download this first; its column headers are what
  BillBasket expects (name, price, tax rate, opening stock, etc.). Typing
  data into a spreadsheet with different column names or order will fail
  to map correctly.
- **Import file** — your filled-in copy of the template, uploaded back
  in. BillBasket reads every row and creates the matching records in one
  pass.
- **Master vs. transactional data** — this importer is for master data
  (products, customers, suppliers, opening stock) that exists before any
  billing happens. It isn't for importing historical invoices or
  transactions — those are created going forward through normal billing.

## Try it: Sharma Kirana Store

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Data Import/Export">
    Rajesh Sharma has a spreadsheet of his existing products from his old
    paper ledger. He opens **Settings → Data Import/Export** on his
    Windows PC to bring it into BillBasket.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Download the template">
    He downloads the product import template first, so his columns —
    name, price, tax rate, opening stock — line up with what BillBasket
    expects.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the template">
    Rajesh fills in his products, including Parle-G Biscuits at ₹10, Tata
    Salt 1kg at ₹28, and Amul Milk 500ml at ₹32, along with their opening
    stock counts.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload the completed file">
    He uploads the completed file. BillBasket reads it and adds all the
    products at once, ready to bill against immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>
