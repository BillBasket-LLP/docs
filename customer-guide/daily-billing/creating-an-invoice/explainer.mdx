An invoice is the core of daily billing in BillBasket — everything else
(adding items, discounts, tax, payment, printing) happens inside one. Every
invoice you save is stored permanently, so you can always reopen, edit, or
reprint it later from your invoice list.

## The pieces of an invoice

- **Customer** (optional) — pick an existing one, add a new one on the
  spot, or leave it blank for a quick walk-in sale. See [Adding a
  customer](/customer-guide/parties/adding-a-customer/explainer).
- **Line items** — the products/services being sold. See [Adding
  products/services to a bill](/customer-guide/daily-billing/adding-items-to-a-bill/explainer).
- **Discounts and tax** — applied automatically per product, adjustable per
  line or for the whole bill. See [Applying discounts &
  tax](/customer-guide/daily-billing/discounts-and-tax/explainer).
- **Payment** — cash, card, UPI, credit, or split across methods. See
  [Taking payment](/customer-guide/daily-billing/taking-payment/explainer).
- **Print/share** — receipt, label, PDF, or a direct share link. See
  [Printing & sharing bills](/customer-guide/daily-billing/printing-and-sharing/explainer).

## How the pieces depend on each other

Picking a customer isn't required to start a bill, but it unlocks two
things that don't apply to a walk-in sale: **credit** as a payment method
(see [Credit accounts](/customer-guide/parties/credit-accounts/explainer)), and,
where the customer has more than one registered tax number, a **tax
branch** picker at billing time (see [Per-customer tax
branches](/customer-guide/parties/per-customer-tax/explainer)). The invoice's
final total is what payment is collected against — so any discount or tax
change you make has to happen before you take payment, not after.
