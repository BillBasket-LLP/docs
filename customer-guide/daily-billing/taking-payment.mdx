---
title: "Taking payment (cash/credit/split payment)"
description: "Collect invoice payment in cash, card, UPI, bank transfer, or credit, split a bill across multiple methods, and record partial payments against the customer ledger."
---

At checkout, choose how the customer is paying. BillBasket supports
several payment methods on the same invoice, and always shows what's still
outstanding.

## Understanding the fields

- **Payment method** — one of **Cash**, **Card**, **UPI**, **Bank
  transfer**, **Cheque**, **Credit note**, or **Credit**. Each row of
  payment on an invoice picks its own method, so a single bill can be
  split across more than one.
- **Credit** — records the sale against the customer's account instead of
  collecting money now. This method only appears as an option when a
  customer is linked to the invoice and their credit is enabled — see
  [Credit accounts](/customer-guide/parties/credit-accounts). A
  walk-in sale with no customer can't use Credit.
- **Credit note** — pays (fully or partly) using a credit note already
  issued to that customer, instead of new money changing hands.
- **Amount** — how much of the invoice total this particular row covers.
  The remaining, unpaid balance automatically defaults to **Credit** if a
  customer is linked, or stays as an outstanding cash amount if not.
- **Reference number** — for non-cash methods (card, UPI, bank transfer,
  cheque), a place to note the transaction/cheque reference for your own
  records; this field is hidden for Cash and Credit since there's nothing
  to reference.

## Invoice payment status

Every invoice is either fully paid or **partial** — BillBasket doesn't
store a separate "unpaid"/"overdue" status, but reminder messages
calculate days-overdue from the invoice date so you know how long a
partial balance has been outstanding.

<Tip>
  You can collect a pending payment on an existing invoice at any time
  from the invoice list, without creating a new bill.
</Tip>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Choose the payment method">
    At checkout, pick Cash, Card, UPI, Bank transfer, Cheque, Credit note,
    or Credit for this payment.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Split the bill across more than one method">
    Add a second payment row for the remaining amount and pick a
    different method — for example, part cash and part credit for a
    regular customer.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Record a partial payment">
    Enter less than the full amount and finish the sale — the invoice is
    saved with a partial status and the remaining balance recorded
    against the customer, if one is linked.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Collect the rest later">
    Reopen that invoice from the invoice list whenever the customer pays
    the remainder, without creating a new bill.
  </Step>
</Steps>
