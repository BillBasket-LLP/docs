Your dashboard shows a summary of today's sales, outstanding dues, low-stock
items, and other key figures as tiles. It's the first screen you see after
opening BillBasket, so you can size up the shop's day in a glance before
you've billed a single customer.

## Understanding the fields

BillBasket builds the dashboard from a fixed set of blocks. Which ones you
see, and in what order, depends on what you've turned on and how you've
arranged them (see [Customizing the dashboard](/customer-guide/reports-and-dashboard/customizing-the-dashboard)) —
but here's what each block shows and when it appears at all.

- **Getting started** — a one-time banner with setup steps (add a product,
  record a sale, and so on). It's meant to fade into the background once
  you're up and running, but you can hide it any time.
- **Today's Sales** — total value billed so far today. Resets at midnight.
- **Receivables** — the total your customers currently owe you across all
  their running credit balances.
- **Payables** — the total you currently owe your suppliers.
- **Low Stock** — a count of products that have fallen below their reorder
  point, so you know to restock before you run out.
- **Return Stats** — a quick summary of recent returns/replacements, so you
  can spot a pattern (like one product coming back often) early.
- **Business trends** — a combined chart: bars for sales and lines for
  receivables/payables over time. It has its own controls:
  - **Daily / Monthly** toggle — switches the chart between day-by-day and
    month-by-month bars. Switching this re-groups the same date range rather
    than changing which days are included.
  - **Date range picker** — choose the start and end date the chart covers
    (defaults to the last 30 days). This range applies only to the trends
    chart, not to the KPI tiles above it, which always reflect today.
- **Recent Invoices** — your latest bills, so you can jump back into one
  without going through Reports.
- **Quick Actions** — shortcut buttons for common tasks (new bill, new
  purchase, etc.).
- **Ask BillBasket (AI Assistant)** — only shows up if the on-device AI
  Assistant feature is turned on for your install, and only on the main
  counter device — a phone paired to that counter as a companion device
  won't see it, since it opens a settings screen only the counter machine
  has.
- **Connect a device (QR)** — a shortcut into pairing a phone with this PC.
  Like the AI Assistant tile, this only appears on the main counter device,
  not on a paired phone — see
  [Phone and desktop together](/customer-guide/settings/companion-and-web-access).
- **Receivables list / Payables list / Low Stock list** — the detailed,
  scrollable versions behind the Receivables/Payables/Low Stock tiles above,
  for when you want to see every customer or product rather than just the
  total or count.
- **Best Sellers** — your top-moving products, so you know what to keep
  stocked up.

<Note>
  Two blocks — Ask BillBasket and Connect a device — are tied to your
  install's settings and device role, not to your personal show/hide choices.
  If you don't see them, it's because the underlying feature is off or
  you're on a paired phone, not because you hid them.
</Note>

## Try it: Sharma Kirana Store

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open BillBasket in the morning">
    Rajesh Sharma opens BillBasket at Sharma Kirana Store and lands on the
    dashboard, which shows a summary of the shop's current state.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check the Today's Sales tile">
    He glances at the "Today's Sales" tile — since the shop just opened,
    it currently reads ₹0, as expected.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Spot a low-stock warning">
    The "Low Stock" tile shows Parle-G Biscuits is running low, reminding
    Rajesh to reorder soon.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check outstanding dues">
    The "Receivables" tile shows the total customers owe, including
    Meena Devi's running credit balance, giving Rajesh a quick sense of
    what's still to be collected.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Switch the trends chart to Monthly">
    Curious how the shop has done this quarter, Rajesh taps the "Monthly"
    toggle on the Business Trends card and picks a wider date range to see
    sales, receivables, and payables by month instead of by day.
  </Step>
</Steps>
