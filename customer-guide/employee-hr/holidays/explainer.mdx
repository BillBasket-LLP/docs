Keep a list of named holidays — like "Diwali" or "Independence Day" — for
your store. Each holiday you add reduces the payable days counted for that
month when you run payroll, so you don't have to adjust salaries by hand.

<Note>
  Employee HR is an optional add-on module — turn it on from Settings →
  Add-ons (Admin PIN required) if you don't see it in your menu.
</Note>

## Understanding the fields

- **Name** — a label for the holiday, like "Diwali" — this is what shows up
  in the holiday list and in attendance for that date.
- **Date** — the single date the holiday falls on. There's no date-range
  field; a multi-day festival needs one entry per day.
- **Type** — **Holiday** or **Closure**. Both work the same way for payroll
  and attendance; the distinction is just for your own record-keeping (for
  example, telling a planned festival holiday apart from an unplanned
  closure).
- **Active** — a holiday can be deactivated instead of deleted, which
  removes it from future payroll calculations while keeping the historical
  record intact for months where it already applied.

## How holidays affect payroll

The holiday list is per-store and feeds directly into two places:

- **Attendance** — a date you've added here automatically shows as
  **Holiday** in the attendance list for every employee, instead of needing
  to be marked by hand day by day.
- **Payroll** — when you run payroll for a month, any holiday that falls
  inside that month reduces the payable days used to calculate salary — so
  your staff aren't short-changed for a day your store was closed, and you
  don't have to remember to adjust it manually.

<Note>
  This is for one-off, named holidays only. For your store's regular
  *weekly* off day (for example, every Sunday), set that separately under
  [Settings → Non-working Days](/customer-guide/settings/add-ons-and-system/explainer).
  That weekly off day shows up in attendance as **Week off**, distinct from a
  named **Holiday** entry, but both count the same way in payroll.
</Note>
