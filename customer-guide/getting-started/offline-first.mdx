BillBasket is built **offline-first**: every feature — billing, stock,
customers, reports — works with no internet connection at all. Your shop
doesn't stop because your Wi-Fi does.

<Note>
  This page is conceptual — offline-first isn't a screen with fields to set,
  it's how BillBasket is built. There's no "Understanding the fields"
  section here for that reason.
</Note>

## Where your data lives

All your billing, stock, and customer data is stored **encrypted, on your
own device**. Nothing needs to be uploaded anywhere for BillBasket to work.
The database key is tied to your specific computer or phone (not your
Windows version), so an OS update won't lock you out of your own data.

## What still needs internet

A small number of things use the internet when it's available, but never
block your daily billing if it isn't:

- Activating or checking your license
- Optional cloud backup and sync between your devices (if enabled for your plan)
- On-device AI features that call out to cloud AI (only when you explicitly use them)

## What this means day to day

- You can keep billing during a power cut on a charged laptop/phone, or when
  your internet is down.
- Your data isn't lost if you lose signal or your connection is slow.
- Regular local backups are still important — see [Backing up your
  data](/customer-guide/data-and-backup/backing-up-your-data) — since
  offline-first protects you from *no internet*, not from a lost or damaged
  device.

## Try it: Sharma Kirana Store

<Steps>
  <Step title="Keep billing through an outage">
    When the internet goes down at **Sharma Kirana Store** one afternoon,
    Rajesh Sharma keeps creating invoices, adding stock, and looking up
    reports exactly as before — nothing about billing depends on being
    online.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check what's paused instead of blocked">
    Rajesh notices only a couple of things wait for the connection to
    return: his license check and the optional cloud backup sync. Neither
    one stops him from serving customers.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the data stays local and safe">
    Once the internet is back, Rajesh confirms nothing was lost — every
    invoice he created offline was already saved, encrypted, on his own
    computer the whole time.
  </Step>
</Steps>
