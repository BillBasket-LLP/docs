## Try it: Sharma Kirana Store

<Steps>
  <Step title="Pick the customer on a new invoice">
    Rajesh Sharma starts a new bill for a regular wholesale customer that
    operates two registered branches, and picks that customer as usual.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a tax branch instead of retyping the tax number">
    Since this is the customer's first bill in BillBasket, Rajesh adds a
    branch with a label ("Main Branch"), that branch's tax number, and its
    billing address — and marks it as the default.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Switch branches when billing a different location">
    The next month, the same customer orders for their second branch.
    Rajesh adds a second branch ("Warehouse Branch") with its own tax
    number and address, and picks it instead of the default for this
    invoice only.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Past invoices keep the branch that was actually used">
    Later, Rajesh reprints last month's invoice — it still shows "Main
    Branch" and its tax number, even though the customer's default has
    since changed, because the invoice saved a snapshot of what was picked
    at the time.
  </Step>
</Steps>
