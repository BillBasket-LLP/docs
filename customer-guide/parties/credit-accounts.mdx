---
title: "Credit accounts & payment tracking"
description: "Let trusted customers buy on credit in BillBasket — set a credit limit, track outstanding dues, and see a warning when a customer goes over their limit."
---

Every customer can carry a running balance in BillBasket — you don't
need to switch anything "on" to bill someone without full payment
upfront. BillBasket tracks their outstanding balance automatically, and
you can record payments against it at any time, independent of any
single invoice.

## Understanding the fields

These fields live on the customer's own profile (**Parties → customer →
Customer settings** — only shown when the Customer role is on; see
[Adding a customer or
supplier](/customer-guide/parties/adding-a-customer)):

- **Credit limit** — the balance you're comfortable letting this
  customer carry. Setting it to 0 means no limit is enforced at all —
  BillBasket will let their balance grow indefinitely without a warning.
  Setting a limit above 0 doesn't block a sale, but it does change what
  you see next:
- **Outstanding balance** (shown on the party's profile and in the
  party picker while billing) — the running total this customer owes.
  When a credit limit is set and the balance goes over it, BillBasket
  highlights the balance so you notice before extending more credit —
  it's a warning, not a hard stop, so you can still choose to bill them
  anyway.

## Try it: Sharma Kirana Store

<Steps>
  <Step title="Set a credit limit for a trusted customer">
    Meena Devi is a trusted regular, so Rajesh Sharma opens her party
    profile and sets a credit limit he's comfortable with.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Bill her on credit">
    Meena picks up Parle-G Biscuits, Tata Salt, and Amul Milk without
    paying cash. Rajesh bills the invoice against her account, and her
    outstanding balance goes up by that amount.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Track the running balance">
    Over the following weeks, Meena buys a few more times on credit.
    Rajesh checks her party profile anytime to see her running dues total.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Record a payment">
    At month's end, Meena pays off part of what she owes. Rajesh records
    the payment against her account, independent of any single invoice,
    and her balance drops accordingly.
  </Step>
</Steps>
