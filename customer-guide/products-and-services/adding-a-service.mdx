---
title: "Adding a service"
description: "Add a billable service like labor, repair, or installation to BillBasket alongside your products, with its own price, tax rate, and unit of measure — no stock tracking involved."
---

Services work like products for billing purposes but skip stock
tracking entirely — there's nothing to count or run low on. Add one from
**Products → New Service** and it appears in its own Services list, ready
to bill by the session, hour, or job.

## Understanding the fields

The service form is a trimmed-down version of the product form — it
drops everything related to physical stock and adds nothing sold by
weight or count:

- **Name** — what shows on the bill, same as a product.
- **SKU** — your own code for the service. Leave it blank and BillBasket
  auto-generates one when you save, just like on the product form.
  Services skip the Manufacturer and barcode/UPC fields entirely, since a
  service isn't manufactured or scanned.
- **Category** — groups services for reporting, same purpose as on
  products.
- **Unit of measure (UOM)** — how the service is billed: "session,"
  "hour," "job," and so on. The dropdown only offers units scoped to
  services (or to both product and service) — units created for
  product-only use (like "kg" or "liter") won't clutter this list. See
  [Units of measure](/customer-guide/products-and-services/units-of-measure).
- **Selling price and tax rate** — set the same way as on a product; you
  can also mark the price tax-inclusive.
- **No stock, no tracking toggles, no supplier fields** — the service
  form has no opening-stock field, no low-stock alert, no Track Serial
  Numbers or Track batches toggle, and no supplier section, because none
  of those apply to something that isn't held in stock.

## Try it: James Spa

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open New Service">
    James Fernandes runs **James Spa**, a pure service business — no
    products to stock, just treatments to bill. He opens **Products → New
    Service** to add "Swedish Massage."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter name, price, and tax rate">
    He names it "Swedish Massage," sets the price to ₹1,200, and picks the
    applicable tax rate. He repeats this for "Facial Treatment" at ₹800.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the unit of measure">
    Since a treatment is charged per session rather than by weight or
    count, he sets the unit of measure to "session."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Bill a client">
    When his therapist Priya finishes a Swedish Massage for a walk-in
    client, James bills it directly from the service list — no stock is
    deducted, since it's a service line.
  </Step>
</Steps>
