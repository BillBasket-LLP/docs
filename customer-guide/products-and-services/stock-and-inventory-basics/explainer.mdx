Stock updates automatically as you go about your normal day — billing a
sale, recording a purchase, or processing a return all move the number
without any extra effort. For everything else (damage, theft, a physical
count that doesn't match), BillBasket gives you a manual stock adjustment
with a reason attached, so the ledger stays honest.

## Understanding the fields

The Inventory screen's **Adjust stock** dialog is where you correct a
count by hand:

- **Current stock** — shown read-only at the top of the dialog so you
  can see what BillBasket currently believes the count is before you
  change it.
- **Quantity (+/-)** — a signed number: enter a positive number to add
  stock, a negative number to remove it. This is a change, not a new
  total — you're telling BillBasket how much to move the count by, not
  what the count should become.
- **Reason** — a dropdown explaining why you're adjusting: Correction,
  Damage, Theft, or Opening stock. This is required so every adjustment
  leaves a clear audit trail of *why* stock moved outside of a normal
  sale or purchase, not just that it did.

<Note>
  Batch-tracked products can't be adjusted from this dialog — their stock
  comes from their individual batches/lots, so a blanket manual
  adjustment would throw off the batch ledger. For those products,
  correct stock by adding or editing a batch instead.
</Note>
