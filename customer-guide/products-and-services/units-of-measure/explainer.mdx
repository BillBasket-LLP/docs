title: "Units of measure (UOM)"
description: "Set how a BillBasket product or service is sold and measured — pieces, kilograms, liters, or your own custom units — and control which forms offer each unit."
---

Every product or service has a unit of measure (UOM) — for example
piece, kg, or liter. BillBasket ships with common built-in units and
lets you add your own from **Settings → Units**.

## Understanding the fields

When adding a custom unit in **Settings → Units**:

- **Name** — the friendly label shown in dropdowns, like "Session" or
  "Sack."
- **Abbreviation** — the short code actually stored on the product and
  printed on the bill, like "ses" or "bori." Both the name and the
  abbreviation must be different from every other unit you already
  have (built-in or custom) — BillBasket blocks a save that would
  duplicate either one, so you don't end up with two units that look
  or print the same.
- **Scope** — Product, Service, or Both. This controls where the unit
  shows up as an option: a Product-scoped unit only appears on the
  product form, a Service-scoped unit only on the service form, and
  Both makes it available on either. This keeps unrelated units (like
  "liter" for groceries) from cluttering a service form, and vice versa.
  Each unit's scope also shows as a small "P" / "S" / "P·S" badge in the
  Units list so you can tell at a glance where it applies.
