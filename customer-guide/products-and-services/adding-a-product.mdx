---
title: "Adding a product"
description: "Add a new product to BillBasket with SKU, barcode, category, tax rate, unit of measure, and opening stock so it's ready to bill and track through inventory."
---

Every item you sell starts as a product in BillBasket. Add one from
**Products → New Product** and it becomes searchable and scannable at
billing time, tracked in inventory, and included in your reports.

## Understanding the fields

- **Name** — what shows on the bill and in product search. This is the
  only field you truly can't skip.
- **SKU** — your own internal code for the item. Leave it blank and
  BillBasket assigns one automatically when you save, so you never have
  to invent codes by hand.
- **Barcode** — the code a scanner reads. If you turn on **auto-assign
  barcode** and leave this blank, BillBasket generates one from your
  store's barcode series on save; once a barcode is assigned it won't be
  regenerated later, so it's safe to print labels right after saving.
- **Category** — groups the product for reporting and browsing (e.g.
  "Groceries"). Optional, but makes the product list easier to navigate
  as your catalog grows.
- **Manufacturer/brand** — an optional link to a brand, useful for
  filtering and for suppliers who deal in specific brands.
- **Unit of measure (UOM)** — how the product is sold and counted (piece,
  kg, liter, etc.). Defaults to "pcs." See [Units of
  measure](/customer-guide/products-and-services/units-of-measure) for
  how this interacts with billing.
- **Cost price and selling price** — what you paid versus what you
  charge. Only selling price is required; cost price is optional but
  powers margin reports.
- **MRP** — the printed maximum retail price, if different from your
  selling price. Optional.
- **Tax rate** — the tax slab applied on this product's bills. You can
  also mark the price as **tax-inclusive**, in which case BillBasket
  works the tax backward out of the price you entered instead of adding
  it on top.
- **Product type** — Simple (the default, a single stocked item),
  Variants (a parent item with multiple size/color options), or Bundle
  (a set of other products sold together). This choice changes what
  else you can set:
  - **Track Serial Numbers** only applies to Simple products — it's
    switched off and locked for Variants and Bundles, since individual
    serial capture doesn't make sense for a multi-variant parent or a
    bundle of other items.
  - **Track batches/expiry** is available for Simple and Variant
    products but locked off for Bundles, since a bundle's stock comes
    from its component items, not its own lot.
- **Manage stock** — turns inventory tracking on or off for this
  product. When it's off, the product can still be billed, but
  BillBasket won't track a quantity for it (useful for made-to-order or
  non-stock items). Turning it off hides the opening stock and
  low-stock fields entirely, since they wouldn't mean anything.
- **Opening stock** — how many units you currently hold, entered only
  when you first create the product. Once saved, this becomes a
  read-only "current stock" display — later stock changes go through
  sales, purchases, or a stock adjustment instead, so the count always
  has a clear paper trail.
- **Low-stock alert** — the quantity below which BillBasket flags the
  product as low stock in Inventory. Only shown when Manage stock is on.
- **Track Serial Numbers** and **Track batches/expiry** — two independent
  toggles for high-value items sold one-by-one (serial) or perishable/
  lot-controlled items (batch/expiry). Both can be on together for a
  product that needs both. See [Tracking serial
  numbers](/customer-guide/products-and-services/serial-number-tracking)
  for the serial workflow.

## Try it: Sharma Kirana Store

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Products and start a new product">
    Rajesh Sharma has a new stock of Tata Salt 1kg to add before it can be
    sold. He opens **Products → New Product**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter name, price, and tax rate">
    He types "Tata Salt 1kg" as the name, sets the price to ₹28, and picks
    the applicable tax rate and category ("Groceries").
  </Step>
  <Step title="Leave SKU and barcode blank">
    Since the packet doesn't have a scannable code handy, Rajesh leaves the
    SKU and barcode fields blank — BillBasket auto-generates them.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set opening stock and save">
    He enters the opening stock count of 40 packets and saves. Tata Salt
    1kg is now ready to bill and shows up in inventory tracking right away.
  </Step>
</Steps>
